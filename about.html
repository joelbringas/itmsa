Established in 2002, the Information Technology Management Students’ Association (ITMSA) is a non-profit, student-run course union serving students within the Business Technology Management (BTM) program at the Ted Rogers School of Management.

Ryerson’s BTM program is the largest of its kind in North America, with a strong focus on integrating Business and Information Technology. It is the ITMSA’s mission to help bridge the gaps for BTM students in order to ensure they are fully prepared to enter the industry and excel beyond their expectations.

Through hosting numerous case competitions as well as professional and academic events, we hope to foster an environment full of innovation and collaboration, in order to provide the support system necessary to help our students take their development to the next level.

Since inception at Ryerson University in 2005, Connect IT has grown to become one of Canada’s largest undergraduate business technology conferences. Bringing together students on an application basis from leading universities across Ontario, we explore a new emerging trend in technology each year, offering our delegates an opportunity to learn, network, and play. The design of our conference ensures delegates are immersed and engaged with topics encompassing the theme.

For over a decade, Women in Information Technology (WITM) has been striving to bring together professionals, partners, and students, particularly women in Information Technology Management to help break the barriers that exist in STEM fields and the corporate realm.

WITM aims to bridge the diversity gap and eradicate the shortfall of female leaders in said fields, by focusing on the 3 pillars of our mandate (encourage, empower and equip) and also aims to mobilize students with the necessary knowledge and skills to contribute to a socially progressive culture.

Established in 2002, Ted Rogers School of Management's ITMSA has grown to become the premier course union for the largest Business Technology Management (BTM) student population in the country. Our executive team continually strives to seek what is in the best interests for our students in order to improve our program and equip students with the skills they'll need upon graduation. ITMSA hosts many events that not only help shape our students to become industry ready professionals after their academic career, but to also encourage more well rounded, social and corporate responsible individuals.
